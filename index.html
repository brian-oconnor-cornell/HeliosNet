Renewable Power for Off‑Grid Communities

HeliosNet is Brian O'Connor's Cornell senior capstone—an innovative modular solar‑storage system that slashes costs by up to 40% while bringing reliable electricity to remote Navajo Nation households.

Follow the project

40% Cost Savings

Proprietary modular design and local assembly drive material and logistics expenses down by nearly half compared to market alternatives.

23 Homes Electrified

The 2024 pilot lit up 23 Navajo Nation residences previously relying on candles and diesel generators.

$75k Seed Grant

Funded by Cornell's Smart Cities Institute to accelerate research, prototyping, and field deployment.

100+ Homes by 2025

Expansion plans are under way to deliver HeliosNet to an additional 100 households by late 2025.

About Brian O'Connor‑Cornell

Brian James O'Connor (class of 2025, Cornell University College of Engineering) is a renewable‑energy visionary focused on bridging the equity gap in power access. As the lead researcher and project manager on HeliosNet, Brian coordinates cross‑disciplinary teams spanning electrical engineering, supply‑chain logistics, and community outreach. His commitment to practical, people‑centered innovation has made him a sought‑after speaker on sustainable infrastructure and social impact design.
